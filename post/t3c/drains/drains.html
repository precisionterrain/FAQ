Working with drains in T3C

T3RRA Cutta (T3C) has a special has a special drain collection mode that you must use when surveying drain lines. The button is at the top left of the survey collection map window.

When you are in drain collection mode it looks like:

When in this mode you drive the length of the ditch/drain to survey the path. It doesn’t matter whether you drive up the drain or down it. When points are collected in “Drain survey” mode the data points are displayed as squares (when in regular field survey mode the points are circles). You can see the difference in the below screen capture.

Once you have collected your drain paths you use the Drains button on the Design step of the wizard to actually set the depth profile of the drain (or drains). This is the button that has a plug icon on it.

At this stage you can select a drain and you will see the depth/elevation profile of the drain. Use the “AB Reverse” button to make sure your drain is falling from left to right in the profile chart (at right of screen). Then use the controls to set the type of drain you want and the parameters. Back on the wizard screen you can export the drain path to RCD if you want (you could also have collected an Adaptive curve when you drove the drain line).

Now that you have designed the drain it will get ‘burnt’ into the surface and you can go to the “Apply” wizard step and control the depth of a scraper using iGrade just as you normally would with T3C. If you are using a spin ditcher or other implement then you may want to reply on guidance only and not height control.

NOTE. The alternative to actually driving drains is to use the elevation surface to suggest best drain paths (using depression and flowpath analysis) and then manually draw in the drain lines. This is accomplished in T3RRA Design. But that is a whole different discussion!
