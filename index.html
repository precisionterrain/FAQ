What is the search radius when surfacing in T3C and what should it be set to?

When converting discrete elevation points into a continuous surface (we call this ‘surfacing’) we require you to enter a “Search Radius”. To estimate the elevation at any point on the surface which was not directly measured, we look at the surrounding measured points. The distance the surfacing algorithm will look for surrounding points is determined by the search radius. If the swaths of collected data are far apart then the software will need to look far to find enough points to use.

The importance of doing windows updates

How close must swaths be when collecting elevation data?

There is no hard & fast rule for how close consecutive passes should be when collecting elevation data. It depends on the nature of the field you are attempting to characterize. The important thing is to understand that only those areas that are directly driven over are actually measured. Everything in between can only be estimated from those measurements. If the land between passes changes dramatically then this will not be reflected in the final surface.

Effective use of Google Earth previews when importing data

Using Google Earth (GE) to preview your import data in always a good idea. By checking the elevation GE shows against the range of elevation values T3D is showing you can check to see your import units are correct. For instance, if you have told T3D that your data is in feet but GE is showing elevation values for the field that roughly 3 times greater than what T3D is showing then it is probable that your data was actually in meters.
